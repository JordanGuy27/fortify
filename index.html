Biography

Brad is the founder of Fortify Strength & Performance. He graduated from Brock University with a Bachelor of Physical Education Degree (honours) and obtained a Post-Graduate Certificate in Exercise Science for Health and Performance from Niagara College. He is currently certified with the National Strength and Conditioning Association as a Strength and Conditioning Specialist. He also holds the gold standard in exercise science as a Certified Exercise Physiologist through the Canadian Society of Exercise Physiology.

Brad brings his passion and dedication for training every time he steps out onto the training floor. He sets an example for the effort and intensity he wants his athletes to be training at. His goal is to have his athletes feel empowered and believe in their own potential so that they can be the best version of themselves. He believes that the key to any great training regimen is bringing focus, work ethic and the attitude to get better every single day. He instills this in his athlete’s day in and day out because at the end of the day, these characteristics are the foundation of great, successful people.

Fortified Philosophy

Building athletic foundations is what we do. Our mission is to assess the Athlete as a whole, develop their limitations/weaknesses from the ground up and challenge their capabilities to stimulate success. We are extremely passionate about developing athleticism and empowering our athletes so that not only do they have a competitive advantage over their competition but they are confident about their abilities, as well. We are relentlessly driven to provide proven training methods based on the most up-to-date and leading scientific research to ensure each athlete in our family is FORTIFIED strong.

Sport Specific Training

Fortify Strength & Performance creates very precise athletic training programs, covering every critical aspect about the athlete. The programming is specific to the athlete’s current season (in-season, off-season, pre-season), sport, position, age, gender, biological development, and even their anatomical make up. We break down every component including strengthening common sport-related injury sites and improving common movement faults through joint stability and mobility. We challenge our athlete's strength, speed and power through progressive overload techniques, in order to create an appropriate stimulus to generate greater adaptations.
